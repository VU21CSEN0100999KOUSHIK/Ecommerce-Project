Writing Tutorials

How to write descriptively - Nalo Hopkinson

To write descriptively, you need to use vivid language and specific details to paint a picture in the reader's mind. Here are some tips: Use all five senses, Be specific, Use figurative language, Show, don't tell. Instead of telling the reader what something is like, show them.

learn more

The art of the metaphor - Jane Hirshfield

The art of the metaphor is to compare two seemingly unrelated things in a way that creates a new insight or understanding. Metaphors can be used to describe anything, from the most ordinary to the most abstract. They can be used to evoke emotions, create humor.

learn more

The pleasure of poetic pattern - David Silverstein

Poetic pattern gives us pleasure by engaging our senses and emotions. The rhythm, rhyme, and repetition of poetic language can be soothing, stimulating, and even hypnotic. Poetic patterns can also help us to better understand and appreciate the meaning of a poem.

learn more

The power of the placebo effect - Emma Bryce

The placebo effect harnesses the power of the mind to heal the body. A placebo is a fake treatment, but it can still produce real benefits if the patient believes it will work. The placebo effect is a reminder that our minds and bodies are interconnected, and that our thoughts and beliefs can have a powerful impact on our health.

The power of a great introduction - Carolyn Mohr

A great introduction hooks the reader and makes them want to read more. It sets the stage for the rest of the piece and gives the reader a reason to care.
